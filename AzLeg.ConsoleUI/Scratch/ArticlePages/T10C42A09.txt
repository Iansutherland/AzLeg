<p><font color=GREEN>10-11909.</font> <font color=PURPLE><u>Merger, interest exchange, conversion, domestication or division; definitions</u></font></p>

<p>A. A domestic corporation sole may be a party to or otherwise undertake a merger, an interest exchange, a conversion, a domestication or a division by complying with chapter 34 of this title and title 29, chapter 6. </p>

<p>B. If a domestic corporation sole is to be created in a merger, a conversion, a domestication or a division:</p>

<p>1. The only member and director of the corporation must be the person comprising the corporation sole in accordance with sections 10-11901 and 10-11908.</p>

<p>2. The articles of incorporation must be recorded as provided by section 10-11902.</p>

<p>C. For the purposes of this section, references in chapter 34 of this title to &quot;directors&quot; or &quot;members&quot; are deemed references to the person who is a corporation sole and references to a &quot;corporation&quot; are deemed references to a corporation sole.</p>

<p>D. For the purposes of this section:</p>

<p>1. &quot;Conversion&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 4.</p>

<p>2. &quot;Division&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 6.</p>

<p>3. &quot;Domestication&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 5.</p>

<p>4. &quot;Interest exchange&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 3.</p>

<p>5. &quot;Merger&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 2. </p>
