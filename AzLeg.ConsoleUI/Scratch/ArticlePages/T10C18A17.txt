<p><font color=GREEN>10-1817</font>. <font color=PURPLE><u>Merger, interest exchange, conversion, domestication or division; definitions</u></font></p>

<p>A. A domestic close corporation may be a party to or otherwise undertake a merger, an interest exchange, a conversion, a domestication or a division by complying with chapters 11 and 13 of this title and title 29, chapter 6.Â  For the purposes of this subsection:</p>

<p>1. References in chapters 11 and 13 of this title to:</p>

<p>(a) &quot;Directors&quot; are deemed references to managers.</p>

<p>(b) &quot;Shares&quot; are deemed references to capital units.</p>

<p>(c) &quot;Shareholders&quot; are deemed references to investors.</p>

<p>2. References in title 29, chapter 6 to:</p>

<p>(a) &quot;Governors&quot; are deemed references to managers.</p>

<p>(b) &quot;Interest holders&quot; are deemed references to investors.</p>

<p>(c) &quot;Interests&quot; are deemed references to capital units.</p>

<p>B. A plan of merger must be approved by all of the investors.</p>

<p>C. A plan of conversion must be approved by at least two-thirds of the investors.</p>

<p>D. A plan of interest exchange, domestication or division must be approved by a majority of the investors.</p>

<p>E. If a domestic close corporation is to be created in a merger, a conversion, a domestication or a division, the number of investors in the corporation on the effective date of the transaction may not exceed ten investors.</p>

<p>F. For the purposes of this section:</p>

<p>1. &quot;Conversion&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 4.</p>

<p>2. &quot;Division&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 6.</p>

<p>3. &quot;Domestication&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 5.</p>

<p>4. &quot;Interest exchange&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 3.</p>

<p>5. &quot;Merger&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 2. </p>
