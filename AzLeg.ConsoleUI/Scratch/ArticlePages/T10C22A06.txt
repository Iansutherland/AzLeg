<p><font color=GREEN>10-2406.</font> <font color=PURPLE><u>Merger, interest exchange, conversion, domestication or division; definitions</u></font></p>

<p>A. A domestic benefit corporation may be a party to or otherwise undertake a merger, an interest exchange, a conversion, a domestication or a division by complying with chapters 11 and 13 of this title and title 29, chapter 6.</p>

<p>B. If a benefit corporation is to be created in a merger, a conversion, a domestication or a division, the formation of the corporation must otherwise comply with this chapter.</p>

<p>C. If a merger, an interest exchange, a conversion, a domestication or a division would have the effect of terminating the status of a business corporation as a benefit corporation, the plan must be adopted by at least the minimum status vote in order to be effective.</p>

<p>D. For the purposes of this section:</p>

<p>1. &quot;Conversion&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 4.</p>

<p>2. &quot;Division&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 6.</p>

<p>3. &quot;Domestication&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 5.</p>

<p>4. &quot;Interest exchange&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 3.</p>

<p>5. &quot;Merger&quot; means a transaction authorized by title 29, chapter 6, article 2.</p>

<p>6. &quot;Plan&quot; means a plan of merger, interest exchange, conversion, domestication or division. </p>
